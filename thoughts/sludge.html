Sludge and Oil

Our subconsious has considerable influence on reality. It is touted that 90 to 95 percent of some behavior is controlled by our subconscious. Who knows what else! Most like to twist this fact into some deterministic futilism and sure, you certainly could. But there's still that 10 percent, and that 10 percent is what, I figure, hits the subconscious button to "go do that" or "this" and so on. That is, you still choose what to do. Which means you still choose who you are.

How do you choose who you are? You choose by what you pay attention to! Consider all of your memories, the things you know, and how you think you just "know" it by thinking it – consider all of that and then throw in your subconscious into the mix. How is your subconsious involved with your information? It probably absorbs everything. Do you want your subconsious to mingle with magazine headlines or with math problems?

You choose by what you pay attention to!

When you think about how almost every experience or sentence read touches your subsconsious, you can start to picture it as either sludge or oil. It accumulates. Sludge can harden, mold, calcify, degrade and ruin. Oil keeps one nimble and swishes around all ideas, makes connections. Keeps gears moving. With accumulation in mind, one can see the weight behind the adage "can't teach an old dog new tricks". Everything sinks into you and builds up until there's no way to dig that crud out.

So one ought to start managing their sludge and oil. For example, one may "quickly" on Facebook, but that site visit will imprint itself into… your everything, I suppose. Who you are. You become an Facebook User and everything that word means (the general personality found in such places). Your behavior, actions, who you are… all comes from the subconsious. With accumulation in mind, it means that you can't hide who you are. We are what we repeatedly do, as the Greeks knew.
