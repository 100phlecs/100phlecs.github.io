Sludge and Oil

Our subconsious has considerable influence on reality. It is touted that 90 to 95 percent of some behavior is controlled by our subconscious. Who knows what else! Most like to twist this fact into some deterministic futilism and sure, you certainly could. But there's still that 10 percent, and that 10 percent is what, I figure, hits the subconscious button to "go do that" or "this" and so on. That is, you still choose what to do. Which means you still choose who you are.

How do you choose who you are? You choose by what you pay attention to! Consider all of your memories, the things you know, and how you think you just "know" it by thinking it – consider all of that and then throw in your subconscious into the mix. How is your subconsious involved with your information? It probably absorbs everything. Do you want your subconsious to mingle with magazine headlines or with math problems?

You choose by what you pay attention to!

When you think about how almost every experience or sentence read touches your subsconsious, you can start to picture it as either sludge or oil. It accumulates. Sludge can harden, mold, calcify, degrade and ruin. Oil keeps one nimble and swishes around all ideas, makes connections. Keeps gears moving. Now one can see the weight behind the adage "can't teach an old dog new tricks" if the dog is stuck in mental mud. Everything sinks into you and builds up and there's no way to dig crud out.

So one ought to keep sludge and oil in mind. For example, one may "quickly" glance at their phone, but that screen unlock will form another droplet to merge. It's up to you whether or not that golden droplet is olive or molasses. Your behavior, actions, who you are… all comes from the accumulated days, residing in the subconsious. Which means you can't hide who you are, manifested from what you've been. But you can build who you want to be, today. We are what we repeatedly do, as the Greeks knew.
