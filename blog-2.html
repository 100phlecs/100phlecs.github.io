Decisions...

Decisions, decisions... Endless torrent of them. Since there are so many, I'm reducing them. By reducing events, opinions, or things the world comes a lot easier.

With events, well, it could include obligations, movements, travels, activities... everything comes with a timeblock. Instead of wavering between all of them, I just choose wisely which ones leave me energized. So for places and activities, I choose three or less, otherwise I'm not doing anything right. No more wrestling yourself to get out of bed.

People and their opinions are faucets for decision overload. And confusion. The net is scary this way. I would contend people are more influenced by pseudo-anonymous opinions online than people in person. Yet often people sort their online interactions as insignificant. Worth reconsidering, otherwise the algorithms steer you in strange ways. So I limit contact with opinions by blocking YouTube sections or social sites, news sites, etc. This isn't to say that the internet is a failure though: there are nuggets of not-so-random blogs out there worth the time. Just choose wisely!

All of the stuff you got adds to decision fatigue as well. They become binds sometimes. How many things do you rely on? What are you spending your money on? How many points of failure do you have? Reducing clutter, reducing products, reducing software, reducing miscellaneous bookshelf sitters. What you read, how you perform tasks. The less things there are, the less abstract things there are, the less steps needed to perform, the less there is to choose. No more decisions!

Right now I'm trying to reduce everything I can and I find it leaves me less wistful or confused. Hope you give some thought to it as well :).
